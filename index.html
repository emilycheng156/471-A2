White Hat Visualization

This visualization shows complaint outcomes across all groups using the same baseline for comparison. Each group’s outcomes are broken down as a percentage of that group’s total complaints, so you can fairly compare how outcomes are distributed. The main takeaway is that most complaints are unsubstantiated, and this pattern looks similar across different groups when you look at the full picture.

Black Hat Visualization

This visualization is misleading because it uses percentages of the overall dataset instead of percentages within each group. In other words, it shows how much each group contributes to all unsubstantiated complaints, rather than what percentage of that group’s complaints are unsubstantiated. For example, if there are more total complaints from one group, that group will naturally make up a larger share of all unsubstantiated cases, even if their actual rate is similar to others. This makes it seem like that group has worse outcomes, when it’s really just a result of having more complaints overall. The main takeaway is that changing what you divide by—each group’s total vs the overall total—can completely change how the data looks.
